Business vs Hobby: The Nine-Factor Test

The IRS uses these factors to determine if you're a business:

  1. ‚úì Carry on activity in businesslike manner
  2. ‚úì Time and effort indicates profit intent
  3. ‚úì Depend on income for livelihood
  4. ‚úì Losses are due to startup or beyond control
  5. ‚úì Change methods to improve profitability
  6. ‚úì You have knowledge to make it profitable
  7. ‚úì Made profit in similar activities before
  8. ‚úì Activity makes profit in some years
  9. ‚úì Expect assets to appreciate

Key: Show profit in 3 of 5 years = presumed business

Safe Harbor Rule

Avoid penalties by paying the lesser of:

  • 90% of current year's tax liability, OR
  • 100% of prior year's tax (110% if AGI >$150K)

üí° Pro tip: Pay 100% of last year = no penalties guaranteed!

Year-End Tax Strategies

  • Equipment: Buy before Dec 31 for Section 179 write-off
  • Prepay expenses: January rent, insurance, software
  • Inventory timing: Delay purchases if high profit year
  • Accelerate sales: Clear old inventory if low profit year

Section 179 vs Depreciation

  • Section 179: Deduct full cost in year 1 (up to $1,220,000 for 2025)
  • Bonus Depreciation: 60% in 2025 (phasing out)
  • Regular MACRS: Spread over 5-7 years

Qualifying equipment: Computers, printers, cameras, shelving, vehicles (limits apply)

Self-Employed Health Insurance

  • Deduct 100% of health insurance premiums
  • Includes spouse and dependents
  • Above-the-line deduction (reduces AGI)
  • Can't exceed business profit

When to Consider S-Corp
