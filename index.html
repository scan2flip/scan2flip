Flipping 101

A Complete Beginner-to-Pro Playbook

Welcome to Flipping 101! If you're looking to turn everyday items into extra cash—or even a full-time income—this guide is your roadmap. Flipping, also known as reselling, involves buying undervalued items and selling them at a higher price for profit. It's a flexible side hustle that anyone can start with minimal investment, but success requires research, strategy, and persistence.

Quick Start: Your First 30 Days

Week 1 – Setup & Skill Reps

  • Create seller accounts: eBay, Facebook Marketplace, OfferUp, Mercari, Poshmark (choose 2–3 to start).
  • Tools: phone camera + daylight, tape measure, small scale (to 10 lb), printer + 30-up labels (Avery 5160 or thermal).
  • Learn comps: spend 60 minutes a day pulling 20 “sold” comps in your target niche (shoes, small electronics, tools, toys, vintage).
  • Sourcing reps: hit 2 thrift stores + 1 garage sale; buy 5 items with expected ROI ≥ 60% and gross profit ≥ $15.

Week 2 – List & Ship

  • Photo booth: neutral backdrop, 8–12 photos per item (all angles + flaws + tags/labels + measurements). Short 10–20s video when allowed.
  • Titles: Brand + Model + Size/Spec + Keywords (max signal, no fluff). Fill item specifics.
  • Pricing: price at or just below last 90-day sold comp; enable offers.
  • Shipping: under 1 lb → poly mailer; fragile → box + void fill; oversize → weigh & measure before listing.

Week 3 – Routines + Cash Flow

  • Daily: 10 new listings or 10 relists. Respond to offers fast. Pack same day.
  • Track: SKU, buy price (COGS), list price, sale price, fees, ship cost, net, ROI, days to sell. (Spreadsheet or Airtable.)
  • Reinvest 100% of profits into faster-moving inventory.

Week 4 – Niches & Scale

  • Pick 1–2 winning niches (e.g., sneakers, small kitchen appliances, vintage hats, tools, sealed LEGO, video games).
  • Add a sourcing route: estate/moving sales, bin stores, local auctions, gov surplus.
  • Start a simple SKU/bin system and cross-list with a tool (Vendoo/List Perfectly) once your workflow is smooth.

IRS 101

⚠️ IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER ⚠️

This information is for educational purposes only and is not a substitute for professional tax advice. These are common deductions, but may not apply to your specific situation. Always consult with a qualified tax professional.

Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)

This is the #1 deduction. It's the original price you paid for the items you sold. If you bought a video game for $5 and sold it for $40, your COGS is $5.

Shipping Costs

You can deduct the full cost of postage and shipping fees you pay to send items to buyers.

Business Mileage

Track the miles you drive for your business, such as trips to the post office or to source inventory. Use the Mileage Tracker in this app! The IRS sets a standard rate per mile each year.

Supplies

Boxes, bubble wrap, packing tape, printer ink, and labels are all deductible business expenses.
